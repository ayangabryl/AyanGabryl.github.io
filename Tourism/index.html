Bohol History

Pre-colonial era: Bohol was inhabited by indigenous tribes such as the Pintados and the Eskaya.

Spanish colonization: In 1565, Miguel López de Legazpi arrived in Bohol and made a treaty of friendship with the chieftain Datu Sikatuna, known as the Blood Compact.

American period: Bohol became a province of the Philippines in 1901 during the American colonial period.

World War II: Bohol was occupied by the Japanese during World War II and was later liberated by American and Filipino troops.

The Chocolate Hills: The unique geological formation of over 1,000 cone-shaped hills that turn brown during the dry season.

Bohol Earthquake: In 2013, a 7.2 magnitude earthquake struck Bohol causing significant damage and loss of life.
